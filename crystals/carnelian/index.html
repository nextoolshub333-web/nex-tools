What is Carnelian?

Carnelian was worn by Egyptian pharaohs and queens (including Cleopatra) for protection in the afterlife and for creative fertility. Roman warriors carried Carnelian into battle for courage. Medieval European traditions used Carnelian to restore the voice of shy speakers and to cool the temper of overly aggressive ones. Napoleon carried a Carnelian seal throughout his military campaigns. The stone's warm orange color has been associated with the sun, blood, and vital life force across every civilization that has worked with it.

Carnelian is a variety of chalcedony (cryptocrystalline quartz). It forms in volcanic and sedimentary environments worldwide, with major sources in India, Brazil, Madagascar, Russia and Uruguay. The iron content determines the depth of the orange color; heat treatment intensifies color in some commercial Carnelian.

How do I know if Carnelian is real or fake?

Carnelian authenticity can be verified by: hardness testing (should match Mohs 6.5 to 7 (Mohs scale)), visual inclusions (natural Carnelian typically has internal variation, not perfect uniformity), weight (genuine crystal has denser feel than glass or plastic), and purchase source (reputable crystal shops and mineral galleries provide provenance).
