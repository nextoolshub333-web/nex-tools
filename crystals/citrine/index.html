What is Citrine?

Citrine is called the Merchant's Stone because of an old tradition of placing Citrine in the cash register, wallet, or financial records of a business to attract abundance and hold onto wealth. Natural Citrine is rare; most commercially sold Citrine is heat-treated Amethyst or smoky quartz, which is still valid but carries a different energetic signature. True natural Citrine comes from the Ural Mountains, Brazil, Madagascar and France. Its golden color has been associated with sun gods, abundance deities and manifestation magic across cultures for thousands of years.

Natural Citrine forms when Amethyst is heated inside the Earth by nearby volcanic activity over geological time. Commercially heat-treated Citrine uses the same process in a kiln. Both forms work energetically; purists prefer natural Citrine for its fuller frequency range.

How to Cleanse Citrine

Cleansing clears accumulated energetic debris from Citrine. Cleanse after any heavy emotional use, after difficult conversations, after illness, and at minimum once per month as maintenance. Three primary methods:

WATER CLEANSING

Safe for running water. 30 to 60 seconds under cool water is standard. Citrine is also traditionally cleansed by passing through a flame briefly (not recommended for beginners).

SMOKE CLEANSING

Sage, palo santo, or frankincense smoke. Frankincense is especially resonant with Citrine's solar energy.

MOONLIGHT CLEANSING

Citrine does not require frequent cleansing; traditionally, it is said to be self-cleansing due to its high vibrational nature. Occasional moon baths still recharge it.

How do I know if Citrine is real or fake?

Citrine authenticity can be verified by: hardness testing (should match Mohs 7 (Mohs scale)), visual inclusions (natural Citrine typically has internal variation, not perfect uniformity), weight (genuine crystal has denser feel than glass or plastic), and purchase source (reputable crystal shops and mineral galleries provide provenance).
