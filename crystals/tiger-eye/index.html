What is Tiger Eye?

Tiger Eye has been worn as a protective amulet by Roman soldiers, Egyptian pharaohs, and ancient African warriors for thousands of years. The stone's distinctive chatoyancy (the silky golden-brown banding that seems to shift when the stone moves) was believed to contain the eye of a watchful protector. Modern crystal work honors Tiger Eye as the stone of courage, confidence, and the practical willpower needed to translate vision into decisive action. Unlike the raw creative fire of Carnelian, Tiger Eye is the disciplined fire of sustained follow-through.

Tiger Eye forms when quartz slowly replaces the crocidolite (blue asbestos) fibers in the parent rock, preserving the fibrous structure while adding quartz's hardness and optical properties. The chatoyancy is the light reflecting off these preserved fiber structures. South Africa is the primary source, with additional deposits in Australia, Brazil, India and Myanmar.

How do I know if Tiger Eye is real or fake?

Tiger Eye authenticity can be verified by: hardness testing (should match Mohs 6.5 to 7 (Mohs scale)), visual inclusions (natural Tiger Eye typically has internal variation, not perfect uniformity), weight (genuine crystal has denser feel than glass or plastic), and purchase source (reputable crystal shops and mineral galleries provide provenance).
