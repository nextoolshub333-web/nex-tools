Card Symbolism and Imagery of The Wheel of Fortune

A great wheel turns in the sky. At each corner, one of the four living creatures of Ezekiel reads a book - man, eagle, lion, bull, the fixed signs of the zodiac. A snake descends the left side (the fall), a jackal-headed figure ascends the right (the rise), and a sphinx with a sword sits steady on top (the witness). On the wheel are Hebrew letters that spell YHVH and alchemical symbols for the four elements. The wheel is the mechanism of cycles. The sphinx is the consciousness that does not turn with the wheel.

The key visual elements of The Wheel of Fortune in the Rider-Waite-Smith tradition include: large wheel with four creatures at the corners (man, eagle, lion, bull), snake descending, anubis ascending, sphinx on top, hebrew letters (yhvh) and alchemical symbols. Each of these symbols carries layered meaning drawn from Kabbalah, Hermetic philosophy, Christian mysticism, and the esoteric tradition that Arthur Edward Waite synthesized with Pamela Colman Smith in 1909. When reading The Wheel of Fortune, let your eye rest on each symbol before you interpret the card. The card speaks first through image, then through word.

Modern tarot readers often pair the visual reading with a body-level response. Before you consult any interpretation, take a breath and notice what your body does when you see The Wheel of Fortune. Does it expand, contract, lean in, lean back? That somatic signal is the first draft of your reading. The prose that follows fills in the nuance.

The Wheel of Fortune Upright Meaning

The Wheel of Fortune upright is the great turning point. Something is changing that you did not choose and cannot control - a job ending, a move, a reunion, a global shift, an opportunity falling in your lap. The sphinx on top is your invitation: do not fight the turning. Be the witness who sees the full wheel rather than the small piece you were clutching. When the wheel turns, your job is not to stop it. Your job is to climb from rider to witness.

When The Wheel of Fortune lands upright in a reading, the card's core teachings of change, cycles, destiny, turning point, karma are the honest signal. This is not a time to second-guess the guidance. The upright position of any Major Arcana card is the clearest expression of its archetype. Your body will often know this instantly - the card will feel like a soft yes, a sudden clarity, or a confirmation of something you already sensed.

Traditional tarot readers look at the card's position in the spread. The Wheel of Fortune upright in the present position describes current energy; in the future position, incoming energy; in the advice position, the recommended response. Across all positions, the upright teaching of The Wheel of Fortune remains: align with change and let the card's medicine move through you rather than around you.

The Wheel of Fortune Reversed Meaning

Reversed, The Wheel warns of resistance to a cycle that is trying to complete, or a stretch of apparent bad luck that is actually old karma clearing. Sometimes reversed means you are at the bottom of the wheel, tempted to believe this is the end. The wheel keeps turning. The bottom is not forever. The top is not forever either.

A reversal in tarot is not a cancellation of the card. It is the shadow, the blockage, or the distortion of the same energy. The Wheel of Fortune reversed still teaches change, but from the angle of what is missing, excessive, or inverted. The medicine is usually not to avoid the card's theme but to meet it more honestly.

Many modern readers do not distinguish between upright and reversed positions, reading the card's full spectrum in every draw. Others take reversals seriously as a signal of shadow work. Both approaches have wisdom. If The Wheel of Fortune appears reversed and the reading feels heavy, treat the reversal as an invitation to journaling and deeper inquiry rather than as a verdict.

The Wheel of Fortune in Love and Relationships

UPRIGHT IN LOVE

In love, The Wheel upright often signals a fated shift in a relationship - a reunion after years, a sudden meeting, a relationship that was dormant coming back online. It can also mean a breakup that was always going to happen is finally happening. Either way, the wheel is moving.

REVERSED IN LOVE

Reversed in love, The Wheel can mean a relationship stuck in a painful cycle, or resistance to a relationship ending or beginning. It can also signal external forces (family, finances, distance) affecting the love life heavily. The medicine is acceptance of what is actually turning.

In love readings, The Wheel of Fortune often shows up during transitional moments in relationships - the meeting, the clarifying, the deepening, or the ending. The card does not usually tell you what to do; it tells you what the situation is asking of you. For singles, pulling The Wheel of Fortune is frequently a mirror of your current readiness. For couples, it is usually a reflection of the dynamic you are currently co-creating. The card changes as you change.

The Wheel of Fortune for Career and Money

UPRIGHT IN CAREER

Upright in career, The Wheel signals a career pivot that arrives rather than one you planned. The layoff that becomes the business. The phone call that redirects your decade. Favors anyone in entrepreneurship, investment, or roles where timing and luck intersect with skill.

REVERSED IN CAREER

Reversed in career, The Wheel can signal a rough patch - a project collapsing, a contract lost, a market shift unfavorable. The remedy is patience and the long view. Every wheel turns again. Use the dip to consolidate rather than to panic.

In career and financial readings, The Wheel of Fortune often signals a specific archetypal moment: a decision point, a breakthrough, a plateau, or a turning point. Spiritual Message of The Wheel of Fortune

Spiritually, The Wheel is the reminder that you are not the wheel. The wheel of fortune, the wheel of samsara, the wheel of biological life - all of it keeps turning. Enlightenment is not stopping the wheel. Enlightenment is discovering the part of you that has never been on it.

The Wheel is Fortuna, the Goddess of Fortune, the Fates, the Norns. She is the archetype of destiny, cycle and time. Every human life experiences many wheels - career cycles, relationship cycles, health cycles, spiritual cycles. The sphinx on top is the soul that remembers it has done this before. This archetype is not abstract. It shows up as a quality you can embody in your daily life. The practice is not to study The Wheel of Fortune from a book but to recognize the moments in your ordinary day when the card's energy is alive in you or asking to be.
