What Astrology Is

Astrology studies the relationship between celestial positions and human experience. Your birth chart is a map of where the Sun, Moon, and planets were positioned in the sky at the exact moment of your birth, interpreted as a snapshot of energetic influences present at your first breath. Transits are the ongoing movements of the same celestial bodies, interpreted as changing influences across your life.

Scientifically, peer-reviewed research does not support astrology as a predictive system for individual events. However, the framework is widely used as a contemplative tool for self-reflection, relationship navigation, and timing decisions. Our coverage treats astrology as a psychological and symbolic system useful for reflection, rather than as a deterministic predictor.

Transits: Saturn Return, Mercury Retrograde, Nodes

Transits are the ongoing movements of celestial bodies that interact with your natal chart over time. The most impactful transits in any life include Saturn return (ages 28-30, 58-60), Mercury retrograde (3-4 times per year), and nodal axis changes (every 18-19 months).

Saturn Return 2026

Saturn moves from Pisces into Aries in May 2025 and remains through early 2028. If you were born with Saturn in Pisces (~1993-1996 or ~1964-1967) or Saturn in Aries (~1996-1999 or ~1967-1969), your Saturn return is active now.

Mercury Retrograde 2026

Mercury goes retrograde three times in 2026: February 25 to March 20 (Pisces), June 29 to July 23 (Cancer and Leo), October 24 to November 13 (Scorpio).

Lunar Nodes (North Node and South Node)

The 2026 nodal axis is Pisces (North) and Virgo (South). This configuration emphasizes collective themes of compassion and intuition over perfectionism and over-analysis.
